Concept Cards

These cards are written in simple English, like a normal student answer. Each one covers definition, purpose, working principle, example, advantages, limitations, and applications.

Text Preprocessing

1. Sentence Segmentation

Definition: Sentence segmentation means splitting a paragraph into separate sentences.

Purpose: It helps the computer understand where one sentence ends and the next begins.

Working Principle: The system looks for punctuation marks like full stops, question marks, and exclamation marks to find sentence boundaries.

Example: My name is Ravi. I like NLP. → [My name is Ravi, I like NLP]

Advantages: It makes text easier to process and helps in tasks like translation and summarization.

Limitations: It can fail with abbreviations like Dr. or Mr. and with informal writing.

Applications: Chatbots, text summarization, and document analysis.

Text Preprocessing

2. Tokenization

Definition: Tokenization means breaking text into smaller parts called tokens.

Purpose: It makes the text easier for the computer to read and analyze.

Working Principle: The text is split into words, numbers, or sometimes even characters.

Example: I love NLP. → [I, love, NLP, .]

Advantages: Simple, useful, and needed in almost every NLP task.

Limitations: It may split words wrongly if the sentence has punctuation, slang, or spelling mistakes.

Applications: Search engines, machine translation, and sentiment analysis.

Text Preprocessing

3. Case Normalization

Definition: Case normalization means converting all letters into one case, usually lowercase.

Purpose: It avoids treating the same word as different only because of capital letters.

Working Principle: Words like India, INDIA, and india are all changed into the same form.

Example: Love, LOVE, love → love

Advantages: It reduces confusion and makes word counting easier.

Limitations: Sometimes capital letters matter, like in names or proper nouns.

Applications: Text classification, keyword search, and cleaning input text.

Text Preprocessing

4. Stop word Removal

Definition: Stop word removal means deleting common words that do not add much meaning.

Purpose: It helps focus on the important words in a sentence.

Working Principle: Words like is, the, and, a, and of are removed from the text.

Example: I am going to the market → going market

Advantages: It reduces text size and can improve processing speed.

Limitations: Sometimes stop words are important, especially in meaning-based tasks.

Applications: Information retrieval, topic modeling, and spam filtering.
